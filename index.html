About Me

Hey there! My name is Tyler Young, and I was born and raised in Vancouver, British Columbia. I'm currently studying Computer Science at the University of British Columbia, directing my focuses towards video game development and UI/UX design. I'm particularly interested in what makes games fun for players and how good designs and user experiences can transform a fun game into a memorable experience.

Outside of my studies, I love reading about the newest tech news, particularly anything related to video games, computer hardware, or mechanical keyboards. I'm also a lover of fashion, photography, and design, so you may also catch me browsing the internet looking at new outfits from new designers, portfolios of photographers of varying styles, or clicking on buttons over and over again to watch their pretty animations.

Recent Projects

Todd the Necromancer

Todd the Necromancer is a game created for the Global Game Jam 2016. The player's objective is to combine the correct reagents off of the conveyor belts in order to resurrect the dead necromancer on the table. However, mixed in with the reagents are bombs, which will explode if not disposed of correctly. Failure to keep the room tidy by disposing of unused reagents also causes the player to lose vision due to the amount of pollution these magical reagents release!

Technologies: Phaser.io

Team Members: Nick Irvine, Nancy Huang, Jon Chew, Frank Yan

Down Syndrome Sleep/Wake App

The DS Care App was created during the Apps4Kids Hackathon in order to help parents track and monitor the behaviour of children with down syndrome. The application worked on a simple principle of "Two minutes twice a day", referring to the amount of time it would take to track their sleep/wake patterns and activity.

Technologies: Java (Android), Meteor, MongoDB, HTML, CSS

Team Members: 20+ team members on behalf of the Children's Sleep Network

Food Vendor Tracker

The Food Vendor Tracker is an application that tracks the locations of food trucks in the Vancouver area. This data is parsed from a data set provided by the City of Vancouver, and plots points on a map to show locations. The application also supports searching with auto-complete, aggregate ratings, and user authentication.

Technologies: GWT, HTML, CSS

Team Members: Sebastian Munro, Ryan Lee, Cherry Yen, Linh Phan

Allegro Music Store

The Allegro Music Store emulated the inventory system for a music store. This system handled adding and removing of items from inventory, client-side cookies for customer shopping baskets, and generating sales reports. Both the front end design and back end querying were created as part of this project.

Technologies: MySQL, PHP, HTML, CSS

Team Members: Bruce Li, Michelle Chen, Cecile Leung
